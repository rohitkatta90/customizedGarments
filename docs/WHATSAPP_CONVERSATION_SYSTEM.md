# WhatsApp conversation system

End-to-end WhatsApp communication for a women’s clothing service: **custom stitching**, **alterations**, and **girls’ kids wear** as a secondary line. Use this for staff training, quick replies, or CRM playbooks.

---

## Objective

- Convert inquiries into orders  
- Keep requirements clear  
- Build trust  
- Run operations efficiently  

### Tone and style

- Warm, polite, conversational  
- Professional but not robotic  
- Helpful and solution-oriented  
- Fast and clear  

---

## Operating principles

| Principle | In practice |
|-----------|-------------|
| **One thread, one order** | For a new topic, either continue the same order or say you’re opening a “new request” so nothing mixes. |
| **Confirm before you commit** | Don’t promise a date or price the workshop can’t meet; say you’ll confirm and reply in X minutes. |
| **Structured, not stiff** | Short bullets in summaries; keep greetings human. |
| **Capture before you close** | Don’t send final confirmation until service, references, count, delivery window, and payment path are clear. |
| **Women-first, kids natural** | Lead with women’s custom stitching and alterations; mention girls’ wear as “we also do kids’ (girls)” when relevant. |

### Timing (guidelines)

- First reply to a new chat: **within a few minutes** in business hours; if delayed, open with a brief apology.  
- After you promise “checking”: **15–30 minutes** typical; if longer, send a one-liner update.  
- Status pings: **only when something changes**, not daily spam.

## Stage 1 — First interaction (lead handling)

**Goals:** Greet, identify service, get reference, quantity, preferred date.

### Template A — Generic new inquiry

> Hi [Name] 👋  
> Thanks for reaching out to **[Business Name]** — we specialise in **custom stitching** and **alterations** for women’s wear, and we also take **girls’ kids wear** when it fits your order.  
>  
> To help you quickly, could you share:  
> 1️⃣ Are you looking for **stitching (new piece)** or **alteration**?  
> 2️⃣ **Design / reference** (photo or link) — even a rough idea helps  
> 3️⃣ **How many garments**  
> 4️⃣ **Preferred delivery date**  
>  
> Once I have this, I’ll guide you on next steps and timeline 🙏  

### Template B — Only “Hi” / vague message

> Hi! Lovely to hear from you.  
> Are you looking for **custom stitching** or **alteration** today?  
> If you can send a **reference picture** and **how many pieces**, I’ll suggest the best way forward.  

### Template C — They sent image only

> Thanks for the reference — it really helps.  
> Is this for **stitching** or **alteration**?  
> Also, **how many pieces** and **when would you like it ready**?  

### Template D — Kids (girls) mentioned

> Thanks — we do **girls’ wear** as well alongside our women’s work.  
> Could you confirm **age / size** and whether you have a **reference**? Same details as above (pieces + preferred date) and I’ll check feasibility.

## Stage 2 — Multi-item order handling

**Goals:** One clear structure per item, then a **single consolidated summary**.

### Per-item prompt (repeat as needed)

> **Item [2] — quick details**  
> • Service: **Stitching / Alteration**  
> • Reference: *(photo if not sent)*  
> • Notes: **fabric type, colour, lining, measurements if you have** *(optional but helpful)*  
>  
> When you’re done listing items, type **“That’s all”** and I’ll send one combined summary ✅  

### If they mix stitching and alteration

> No problem — we can do **both in one order**. I’ll list each piece separately in your summary so nothing is missed.  

### Pre-summary check

> Before I summarise:  
> Do you have **any other pieces** to add, or shall I prepare the **full list + next steps**?  

### Summary template (draft, before pricing/dates are final)

> **📋 Your request (draft)**  
> **[Customer name]**  
>  
> **Item 1** — [Stitching/Alteration] — [short description] — Ref: [sent/noted]  
> **Item 2** — …  
>  
> **Preferred delivery:** [date or range]  
>  
> Please check if **anything is missing**. I’ll next share **pricing / timeline** and confirm 🙏  

---

## Stage 3 — Pricing and add-ons

**Goals:** Clear base price, transparent extras, **approval before adding**.

### Opening

> Here’s a clear breakdown so there are **no surprises** 👇  

### Base + line items (structure)

> **Estimate**  
> • [Service] — [Item]: **₹___**  
> • [Service] — [Item]: **₹___**  
> **Subtotal:** **₹___**  
>  
> **Optional add-ons** (only if you choose):  
> • [Lace / embroidery / tassels / padding / dyeing / etc.]: **+₹___** each  
>  
> **Important:** Any add-on is **only added after your approval** — we won’t include extras without a clear “yes.”  

### If unsure what they need

> If you’re not sure about add-ons yet, we can **start with the base work** and discuss enhancements once we see the fabric and fit.  

### Approval ask (before charging extras)

> Would you like to add **[specific add-on]** to **[item]**?  
> Reply **Yes** for that line, or **No** — we’ll keep it simple.  

### Anti-overwhelm rule (internal)

- At most **three** optional bullets per message; offer **Option A / B** when possible.  

---

## Stage 4 — Delivery date confirmation

**Goals:** Preferred date → feasibility → **confirm or suggest alternative**; flag urgent/express.

### If date works (after internal check)

> **Delivery date:** **[Date]** works for us ✅  
> We’ll plan the work accordingly and share confirmation once the order is **locked**.  

### If date needs adjustment

> Thanks for sharing **[date]** — that’s a bit tight with our current workload.  
> The **nearest feasible date** we can commit to is **[alternative date]**.  
> Does that work for you? If it’s urgent, we can discuss **express** (subject to availability + **extra charge**).  

### Urgent / express

> For **urgent** delivery, we may be able to prioritise with an **express fee of ₹___** (if workshop confirms).  
> Shall I check and revert in **[X] minutes**?  

### Internal reminder

Check queue, complexity, fabric availability, and seasonal load **before** replying.

## Stage 5 — Final order confirmation (critical)

**Goals:** Full summary, **terms**, **explicit yes** before work or payment.

### Final confirmation message

> **✅ Order summary — please confirm**  
>  
> **Order ref:** [ORDER-ID] *(or “will assign on confirmation”)*  
>  
> **Items and services**  
> [Bullet list: each item + service + approved add-ons + price]  
>  
> **Delivery:** **[Date]** *(or confirmed window)*  
>  
> **Total:** **₹___**  
> *(Advance / balance as per your policy)*  
>  
> **Please read:**  
> • Minor **design / shade / finish** differences can happen vs reference — we’ll match as closely as practical.  
> • **Approved add-ons** are included as listed.  
> • Once **work begins**, orders **cannot be cancelled** (material and labour allocated).  
>  
> Reply **“CONFIRM”** to proceed, or tell me what you’d like to change.  

### If they hesitate

> Totally fine to pause — tell me what you’re unsure about (**price / date / design**), and we’ll fix it before we start.

### Payment options intro

> • **UPI:** [ID / QR instruction]  
> • **COD:** [if offered + conditions, e.g. on pickup]  
> • **Partial advance:** **₹___** now, **₹___** on **[ready / delivery]**  
>  
> **Order total:** **₹___**  
> **Paid so far:** **₹___**  
> **Pending:** **₹___**  

### After payment received

> Thank you — **₹___** received ✅  
> **Balance pending:** **₹___** *(if any)*  
> Your order is **confirmed** and queued for work.  

### Gentle reminder (polite)

> Just a friendly nudge — **₹___** is still pending to **confirm / complete** the order.  
> Let me know once paid, or if you need a different arrangement.

## Stage 7 — Order status updates

Keep each update **one short block**.

| Status | Template |
|--------|----------|
| **Confirmed** | Your order **[ORDER-ID]** is **confirmed** ✅ We’ve added it to our production queue. |
| **In progress** | Quick update: work has **started** on **[item / order]**. We’ll notify you when it moves to finishing / QC. |
| **Almost ready** | Your order is in **final finishing** — on track for **[date]**. |
| **Ready** | Good news — your order is **ready** 🎉 **[Pickup / delivery / try-on]** as discussed. |

Optional trust line:

> If you need any small tweak before final sealing, tell us **before** we close finishing (where possible).

> **Items:** [short list]  
> **Total:** **₹___**

## Stage 9 — Feedback and review

> We’d love your feedback when you have a minute ⭐  
> **How would you rate your experience (1–5)?**  
> Any **short comment** is optional but very helpful for us.  
> If you’re happy, we’d be grateful if you **share your experience** with friends who love good tailoring 💛  

If the rating is low, move to **complaint handling** (next section).

---

## Stage 10 — Complaint handling and resolution

### Acknowledgement (no blame)

> I’m really sorry this didn’t meet your expectation — thank you for telling us.  
> We’ll **fix this fairly**. Could you share **a photo** and **what feels wrong** (fit / length / finishing)?  

### Solution menu

> Here’s what we can do:  
> **Option 1:** **[Alteration / correction]** — timeline **[X]**  
> **Option 2:** **[Alternative]** — if Option 1 isn’t suitable  
> What would you prefer?  

### Closing after fix

> I’m glad we could sort this out. If anything still feels off, message us — we’re here.  

---

## Stage 11 — Upselling and enhancements (optional)

**Rule:** Suggest **after** they’ve chosen a base direction, not in the first message.

> One optional idea: **[lace / embroidery / lining upgrade]** could elevate this design — **+₹___**.  
> Totally optional — happy to proceed **without** it if you prefer a clean, simple finish.  

**Kids (girls), secondary positioning:**

> If this is for **a little one**, we can keep trims **light and comfortable** — I’ll suggest child-friendly options if you’d like.

## Scenario variations

| Scenario | Approach |
|----------|----------|
| **Price shock** | Break into items; offer **phased work** or **remove add-ons**; never argue. |
| **No reference** | Offer **catalog / past work** or describe + rough sketch; still get **date** and **count**. |
| **Alteration only** | Ask what’s wrong (length, waist, zip, lining), **photos front and back**, **wear date**. |
| **Fabric not with them** | Explain **fabric drop-off** or **purchase assist** if you offer it; otherwise clear **next step**. |
| **Repeat customer** | Shorter greeting; “Same measurements as last time?” if appropriate. |
| **Ghosting after quote** | One polite follow-up after 24–48h: ask whether to **hold the date** or **release** it. |

## Pre-confirmation checklist

Use before sending **CONFIRM** or taking advance payment:

- [ ] Service type per item (stitching vs alteration)  
- [ ] Reference or agreed description  
- [ ] Quantity / full list  
- [ ] Measurements or fitting plan (per your process)  
- [ ] Delivery date **agreed**  
- [ ] Price + **approved** add-ons  
- [ ] Payment path + **advance rule** clear  
- [ ] Terms acknowledged (variation / no cancel after start)
